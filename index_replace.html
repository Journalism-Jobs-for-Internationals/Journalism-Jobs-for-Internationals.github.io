About Us

International journalists struggle with a lack of connections and information when it comes to getting a newsroom job in the US. That’s why we started Journalism Jobs for Internationals. Our target audience is global journalists who want to work here and newsrooms seeking to become more diverse and inclusive.

Our first step is to build an informational platform of newsrooms that hire journalists with visas or will sponsor them. At this point, our list is small. We hope – with your help – to expand it. So if you know of a newsroom or newsrooms that will hire journalists with visas, please enter that information here.

Visa Information

H1B

What is it? H-1B visa is a nonimmigrant work visa that allows U.S. employers to hire foreign workers in the U.S. for a specific period of time.

Who is eligible? The H-1B is available to persons who are offered temporary professional positions with U.S. employers.

Who files the petition? The employer must file a petition on behalf of the prospective employee.

How long does the process take? Two to eight months, depending on different situations.

Approximate cost? Cost ranges from about $3,000 to $12,000, including a premium processing fee.

Documents required: A valid job offer from a U.S. employer, passport, CV, diploma, and more.

O1

What is it? O-1 visa is for individuals who possess extraordinary ability in the sciences, arts, education, business, athletics, or the entertainment industry.

Who is eligible? Individuals with extraordinary ability in their field or those with extraordinary achievements in motion picture or television industries.

Who files the petition? The petition must be filed by an employer or sponsor on behalf of the individual.

How long does the process take? Typically 4 to 6 months.

Approximate cost? Basic fees range from $530 to $1,055, with additional costs for premium processing and other fees.

Documents required: Passport, DS-160, appointment confirmation, and various supporting documents.

J1

What is it? The J-1 visa is for educational and cultural exchange programs.

Who is eligible? Individuals coming to the U.S. as students, scholars, trainees, interns, au pairs, and other similar roles.

Approximate cost? Application fee is $185, and SEVIS fee is $220.

Documents required: DS-2019, SEVIS, DS-160, and interview appointment.

I Visa

What is it? The I visa is for representatives of foreign media, including members of the press, radio, film, and print industries.

Who is eligible? Foreign media representatives engaged in reporting news or events.

Approximate cost? Visa application fee is $185.

Documents required: Passport, DS-160 confirmation page, and other application materials.

L Visa

What is it? The L-1 visa is for intracompany transferees working in executive or specialized knowledge roles.

Who is eligible? Employees of multinational companies being transferred to a U.S. office.

Approximate cost? Costs include I-129 Filing Fee, Fraud Prevention and Detection Fee, and others.

Documents required: Passport, visa application form, and proof of employment with the company.
